⚖️ Đạo đức & Hạn chế

Responsible use guidelines · Known limitations · Ethical considerations

🎯 Intended Use

These datasets are designed for population-level research, policy analysis, and educational purposes. They provide aggregate demographic and neuroeconomic indicators and are not suitable for individual-level clinical decisions or diagnostic purposes.

⚠️ Known Limitations

Data quality varies by country and year. Countries with 'medium' or 'low' quality flags have gaps filled by interpolation or estimation. Venezuela data post-2018 is limited due to restricted official statistics. Missing values are coded as NA and should not be imputed without methodological justification.

🌍 Geographic Scope

Primary coverage is Latin America (11 countries) and Ibero-America. Extrapolation to other regions requires validation. The NCRA Risk Score was calibrated on Latin American demographic structures and may not be valid for other contexts without recalibration.

📊 Statistical Limitations

The NCRA Risk Score is a composite indicator — correlations between its component variables create multicollinearity if all components are used simultaneously in regression models. Use either the composite score OR the individual components, not both.

🔄 Temporal Limitations

Annual data points represent year-end estimates. Intra-year variation is not captured. Projections beyond 2030 carry substantial uncertainty and should be interpreted as illustrative scenarios, not forecasts.

⚖️ Ethical Considerations

These data describe population-level demographic risk, not individual risk. They should not be used to stigmatize populations, justify resource denial, or support discriminatory policies. The NCRA framework measures structural vulnerability, not individual capacity.

🔬 Reproducibility

All analyses are reproducible using the provided Python and R scripts. Results may vary slightly across software versions due to floating-point arithmetic. The reference implementation uses Python 3.10+ with pandas 1.5+.

📝 Citation Requirement

When using these datasets, citation is required per the CC0 1.0 terms (attribution not legally required but strongly requested for scientific integrity). See Citation Templates for correct formats.

📌 Quick Reference

❌ Not appropriate
